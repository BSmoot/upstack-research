TITLE CLUSTER RESEARCH SPECIFICATION (LAYER 3)
Research Context: Technology advisory firm (vendor-neutral, commission-based) creating precision messaging for specific buyer personas. Each title cluster has distinct decision authority, pain points, communication preferences, and buying behaviors that require role-specific outreach strategies.
Output Requirements: Each title agent delivers role-specific research building on Layer 1 horizontal and Layer 2 vertical findings. Outputs drive ABM playbook generation with persona-tailored messaging, objection handling, and outbound sequences.
________________________________________
TITLE AGENT: ROLE-SPECIFIC RESEARCH
Core Question: What is the day-to-day reality, pain points, and decision-making context of this specific role - and what precision messaging resonates with their specific pressures?

Research Vectors:

1.	Role Definition & Responsibilities
o	What are day-to-day responsibilities of this role?
o	What are typical KPIs and success metrics?
o	What is typical career path to/from this role?
o	How has this role evolved in recent years?
o	What scope variations exist by company size?
o	How does this role differ across industries?

2.	Decision-Making Authority
o	What infrastructure decisions does this role have authority to make?
o	Where do they have influence but not final authority?
o	What decisions are outside their scope?
o	Who do they need to convince/collaborate with?
o	What budget thresholds require escalation?
o	How do they build consensus for major purchases?

3.	Pain Points & Pressures
o	What are top 5 challenges facing this role today?
o	What external pressures affect them? (market, competition, regulation)
o	What internal pressures affect them? (budget, resources, politics)
o	What keeps them up at night?
o	How do pain points differ across industries?
o	What creates urgency for seeking outside help?

4.	Communication & Content Preferences
o	How does this role prefer to consume information?
o	What professional communities/platforms do they use?
o	Where do they spend time professionally?
o	When are they most receptive to outreach?
o	What content formats work best? (reports, videos, calculators, webinars)
o	What content triggers engagement vs gets ignored?

5.	Buying Behavior & Vendor Selection
o	How does this role typically engage with vendors?
o	What criteria do they use to evaluate vendors/advisors?
o	How do they validate vendor claims?
o	What triggers them to look for outside help?
o	What's their procurement process involvement?
o	How do they handle unsolicited outreach?

6.	Objections & Concerns
o	What objections does this role typically raise about advisors?
o	What concerns do they have about changing vendors?
o	What past experiences shape their skepticism?
o	What would cause them to walk away?
o	What makes them defensive or dismissive?
o	What risks worry them most about engaging an advisor?

7.	Value Proposition & Proof Points
o	What business outcomes matter most to this role?
o	What value propositions resonate?
o	What proof points validate value to this role?
o	What ROI metrics do they care about?
o	How do priorities differ from other decision-makers?
o	What success stories would they find compelling?

8.	Messaging Precision
o	What language does this role use to describe their work?
o	What terminology resonates vs. turns them off?
o	What tone works best? (peer-to-peer, expert, consultative)
o	How technical should messaging be?
o	What makes them pay attention?
o	What first impression do they need to engage?

Deliverable: Comprehensive role analysis including:
- Executive Summary (5-7 key insights about this role)
- Title Role Profile & Evolution
- Decision-Making Authority Map
- Pain Point Analysis (ranked top 5)
- Communication & Content Preferences
- Buying Behavior Analysis
- Objection Handling Framework
- Value Proposition & Proof Points
- Messaging Guidelines for this role
- Outbound Sequence Design Recommendations
- Cross-Vertical Insights (how this role varies by industry)
- Sources Consulted (full bibliography with dates)
- Research Gaps & Confidence Assessment

CONFIGURED TITLE CLUSTERS

C-Suite (CFO, CEO, COO)
- Titles: CFO, CEO, COO, President, General Manager
- Decision Authority: Final approval for strategic/large investments
- Key Focus: Business outcomes, financial performance, strategic risk

Technology Leadership (CIO, CTO, CISO)
- Titles: CIO, CTO, CISO, VP IT, VP Engineering
- Decision Authority: Primary authority for most infrastructure decisions
- Key Focus: Technology debt, innovation vs stability, talent shortage

IT Operations (VP IT, Director IT, Manager)
- Titles: VP IT Operations, Director IT, IT Manager, Network Manager
- Decision Authority: Tactical decisions, influence strategic
- Key Focus: Vendor management burden, resource constraints, firefighting

Procurement & Sourcing
- Titles: VP Procurement, Director Sourcing, Strategic Sourcing Manager
- Decision Authority: Vendor selection process, contract negotiation
- Key Focus: Cost optimization, supplier management, contract compliance

Business Unit Leaders
- Titles: VP Sales, VP Customer Success, VP Operations, Regional GM
- Decision Authority: Department-level technology decisions
- Key Focus: Business outcomes, speed, minimal IT dependency

EXECUTION CONTEXT

Dependencies:
- Requires completed Layer 1 horizontal research (all 5 agents)
- Requires completed Layer 2 vertical research (all configured verticals)
- Uses buyer journey, competitive, messaging, and vertical findings as foundation

Parallel Execution:
- All title agents execute in parallel after Layer 2 completion
- Staggered start (2s delay) to prevent rate limiting
- Each title agent independent (no cross-title dependencies)

Context Injection:
- Layer 1 context provides horizontal buyer journey and messaging insights
- Layer 2 context provides vertical-specific regulations, pain points, and buying patterns
- Title agent synthesizes both to create role-specific precision messaging

Output Usage:
- Direct input to playbook generation (vertical x title combinations)
- Drives ABM campaign content and sequences
- Informs sales enablement materials and talk tracks

Methodology:
- Use 30-40 web_search operations per title cluster
- Prioritize: LinkedIn discussions, professional surveys, role descriptions, interview transcripts
- Focus on LIVED EXPERIENCE not theoretical role definitions
- Look for day-in-the-life content
- Find what actual people in these roles say about their challenges
- Compare across industries where data available
- Every major claim needs source citations
- Flag low-confidence areas explicitly
