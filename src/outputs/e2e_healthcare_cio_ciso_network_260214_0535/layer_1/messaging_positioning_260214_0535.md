## **MESSAGING & POSITIONING RESEARCH: KEY FINDINGS**

### **1. LANGUAGE THAT WORKS (Buyer Voice)**

**Buyer Pain Language (Direct Quotes from Research):**

When organizations lack vendor selection criteria checklists, they struggle to compare options objectively, leading to extended evaluation periods, missed deadlines, and mounting frustration across IT, procurement, and business stakeholders.

42% of buyers struggle to identify the right software for their business, and 60% of buyers regret a purchase they've made in the past 12 to 18 months.

Research shows that enterprises overpay for 85% of their IT purchases.

**Effective Framing for Vendor-Neutral Advisory:**

The research reveals that successful advisory messaging should emphasize:
- **Structural independence** (not "performance-based" claims)
- **Operational transparency** about conflict-of-interest management
- **Measurable buyer outcomes** rather than advisor features

Manufacturers engage research firms because they desire a selection process free from bias and recognize that vendors have natural bias to their own products, while system integrators have bias towards products with which they are familiar, and users simply recognize they "don't know what they don't know."

### **5. OBJECTION PATTERNS: WHAT WORKS**

**"Why not go direct to vendors?"**

Research shows this is defeated by: quantified cost disparities, evidence of vendor unawareness of alternative solutions, and documented time-to-decision speedup.

**"How do you stay neutral if vendors pay you?"**

Successful answer = transparent commission disclosure + structural accountability (tied to post-implementation success, not deal closure).

**"We have internal IT - why do you need you?"**

This needs reframing: You're not replacing IT, you're augmenting procurement discipline across IT's vendor lifecycle - an area

effective vendor evaluation and selection requires deep understanding of the organization's IT needs as well as vendor landscape and market trends, with IT executives ensuring the process is rigorous, transparent, and well-documented.

### **6. ANALOGY & METAPHOR SUCCESS**

**What Works:**
- "**Like a financial advisor for infrastructure**" (aligned incentives, fiduciary language)
- "**Procurement specialist, not broker**" (shifts from transactional to consultative)
- "**Technology concierge**" (ongoing relationship, problem-solving)

**What Fails:**
- "Like a real estate agent" (too transactional, buyer skepticism)
- "Buying agent" (too simplistic, lacks strategic positioning)
- "Trusted advisor" (overused, lacks differentiation)
