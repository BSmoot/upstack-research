### 1. INITIAL ENGAGEMENT PATTERNS

#### What Services Do Customers Buy First?

For professional services firms, initial engagement typically involves technical staff meeting with prospects to discuss a specific project, with conversations turning to requests for proposals or specific service proposals.

In advisory contexts, initial projects are typically:
- **Technology Assessment & Evaluation**: Discovery engagements mapping current infrastructure, vendor relationships, and business objectives
- **Single-Category Evaluations**:

First projects focus on specific high-impact needs like networking, security, or infrastructure assessment, with expansion occurring through upsells and add-ons within the same vendor relationship

#### Engagement Sizes

The largest proportion of B2B buyers spend $11k to $50k annually on their latest technology purchase, indicating that initial advisory engagements typically start within modest scope but with multi-year implications.

A detailed engagement plan should outline how support will be provided at each point in the customer journey, determining the frequency of check-ins, type of content shared, and channels used.

#### How Initial Projects Set Up Expansion

Successful advisory models involve providing ongoing support options that discuss potential follow-up support or additional services that may benefit the client in the future, through regular check-ins or further consulting on new challenges.

Footprint expansion depends on stellar relationships with the parts of the organization you are currently serving, meaning first-engagement trust is foundational to multi-year advisory growth.

#### Expansion Predictors

Cross-sell and upsell opportunities should be identified through exploring opportunities to expand or renew contracts while nurturing client loyalty, trust and satisfaction, through expressing appreciation, recognition and gratitude, and by asking for referrals, testimonials or reviews.

When you already have a relationship with a B2B customer, they'll be more open to discussing additional opportunities, especially if your customer success team has done a good job of servicing them and maintaining contact.

### 2. EXPANSION OPPORTUNITIES

#### What Additional Services Get Purchased

The proportion of ARR coming from expansion revenue—upselling, cross-selling, and price increases from existing customers—increased from 28.8% in 2020 to 32.3% in 2023, highlighting growing importance of customer retention and maximizing the value of each customer relationship, rather than solely focusing on acquiring new customers.

Typical expansion patterns follow:
- **Horizontal Expansion**: Adding adjacent technology categories to existing relationships (e.g., networking client expanding to security or infrastructure)
- **Vertical Deepening**: Deeper engagement within same category through new departments or geographies
- **Lifecycle Extensions**: Renewals bundled with advisory on modernization, consolidation, or strategic shifts

#### Typical Expansion Path

For professional services, post-sale engagement fosters the ability to provide additional services; creating content that will be important to clients in the future (months to years down the road) using tools like monthly or quarterly newsletters for sharing newly created content to create new opportunities.

Capturing more share of wallet with someone already doing business is about earning more business and bigger deals across different departments, business functions, or geographies of an existing customer; footprint expansion depends on stellar relationships with the parts of the organization you are currently serving.

#### Time Between Initial and First Expansion

Research indicates:

For some clients, additional service opportunities emerge within months; for others, a year or more may pass

Regular check-ins from your consultant(s) keep clients apprised of engagement progress, with your project manager regularly asking for feedback; clients can also reach out to project teams with questions

#### What Triggers Expansion

To identify and foster upselling opportunities with existing customers: use data from analytics platforms to identify customers with upsell potential based on usage patterns; create targeted account-based marketing campaigns for specific customer segments with relevant upsell offers; share success stories and case studies highlighting how other customers benefited from additional features or higher-tier plans; offer trial periods for advanced features.

Understanding the customer lifecycle helps: using data to better understand how customers mature in using your products or services is key to providing information needed to hit them at the right time with potential expansion offers.

### 3. CROSS-SELL & UPSELL PATTERNS

#### Common Service Combinations

Cross-selling involves promoting complementary products to customers; for example, Microsoft can cross-sell Microsoft Defender products for security, Microsoft Copilot for AI assistance, Microsoft Authenticator for two-factor authentication.

Advisory firms follow similar patterns:
- **Problem-Solution Bundling**: Clients evaluating one category (e.g., "we need network refresh") simultaneously addressed on security, vendor consolidation, and infrastructure modernization
- **Stakeholder Expansion**: Different departments/geographies introduced to advisory relationships established in adjacent functions

#### How Scopes Expand

Cross-sell definition (from Forrester): the creation of demand in a new buying group within an account; if your customer is based in the U.S., this new group could be in a different geographical sector (APAC or EMEA) or departments.

Strategic analysis of user behavior, preferences, and service usage patterns can pinpoint areas where additional value can be provided; upsell opportunities include spotting features or service plans that fit customer requirements; complementary products can be cross-sold by offering complementary products or services; customized solutions can be tailored based on customer requirements.

#### What Drives Willingness to Expand

Personalization is key to more effective B2B sales; the more you can segment your customers and personalize your approach, the more effective marketing efforts will be.

Pitching upgrades or cross-selling to existing customers generates more revenue for your business and is easier than converting new customers because past buyers know your product works and trust your business.

### 4. CUSTOMER LIFETIME VALUE ANALYSIS

#### CLV Progression

Customer Lifetime Value is the total value of a customer over their lifetime of relationship with a business; SaaS businesses calculate CLV by multiplying ARPU by the number of years they are expected to be customers.

For advisory relationships:
- **Year 1**: Initial discovery/evaluation + first project implementation (typically 1-3x initial contract value)
- **Years 2-4**: Expansion through adjacent categories + renewal with scope increases (3-5x initial engagement)
- **Years 5+**: Strategic partnership with multiple touchpoints + referral generation (5-10x+ initial investment)

B2B customers generate higher lifetime value due to the long-term nature of business relationships and larger transaction amounts; companies provide ongoing support, maintenance, and upgrade opportunities, resulting in consistent revenue streams over extended periods.

#### High vs. Low LTV Predictors

**High LTV Indicators:**

Companies with formal customer success teams retain customers at higher rates and close more expansion revenue

Companies with strong customer retention grew at least 1.8 times faster than their competitors

- Active engagement in advisory moments (evaluations, consolidation projects)

**Low LTV Risks:**

Over 20% of voluntary churn is linked to poor onboarding; the first 30 days determine retention paths

- Lack of ongoing value documentation or client success validation

Competing budget priorities, internal champion turnover, failure to recognize and communicate ongoing value, and changing business requirements

#### Relationship Duration

When it comes to customer retention, relationships matter; this is especially true for B2B professional service firms; customer retention depends on building a reputation for delivering high-quality services and building long-term relationships with customers.

UPSTACK's **3.5-year average client tenure** and **99.8% retention** indicate advisory relationships compound over time, with multi-year engagements enabling strategic positioning that predicts expansion revenue.

### 5. RETENTION & CHURN ANALYSIS

#### Typical Retention Rates

The media and professional services industry has the highest retention rate at 84%.

The professional services industry—which includes consulting firms, legal services, and accounting businesses—boasts an impressive average customer retention rate of 84%; this high CRR can be attributed to the deep, trust-based relationships.

For advisory specifically:

Retention is structurally higher in IT and infrastructure services due to long-term contracts and embedded client relationships: 83% retention is the average for managed IT service firms in 2025; churn hovers around 17%, often tied to contract expiration or M&A

#### Why Customers Churn

42% of buyers reported poor customer service from their current vendor as a trigger to seek out alternatives, highlighting the importance of exceptional client support in retaining B2B customers.

Primary churn drivers:
1. **Onboarding/Implementation Failures**:

A weak onboarding process is still the top predictor of churn; in fact, over 20% of voluntary churn is linked to poor onboarding; the first 30 days are where retention paths are set

2. **Value Communication Gaps**:

Failure to recognize and communicate ongoing value is a key retention challenge

3. **Organizational Transitions**: Champion turnover, budget reallocation, organizational restructure

#### Churn Risk Predictors

A customer health score can help customer success managers and teams identify risks before they arise and unhappy customers, helping them minimize the churn rate.

Track user behavior metrics (usage frequency, login activity, feature adoption and engagement, support ticket trends) to identify early signs of risk; develop a scoring system to assess customer health based on usage, engagement, satisfaction; use your scoring system to prioritize intervention; implement an early warning system that flags accounts showing signs of potential risk; set up automated alerts for key metrics to enable timely intervention.

#### Churn Prevention Strategies

Part of building a collaborative partnership is staying in close contact; it's important to establish clear communication channels and schedule regular check-ins with active vendors, "to understand performance, expectations, and progress while recognizing that no process or service goes perfectly all the time"; it is also important to involve your key vendors in strategy and planning sessions related to their services or products as this "deepens and elevates your relationship into a strategic partnership".

Beyond delivering the project, enduring client synergy involves providing sustained support, value-added services, and exploring opportunities for further collaboration; nurturing client loyalty through appreciation, recognition, and proactive communication enhances satisfaction; the emphasis on seeking referrals and testimonials contributes to a positive reputation, fostering a strong and profitable consultancy business.

To grow your business, you need satisfied customers or 'promoters' who talk about you enthusiastically and send referrals your way.

UPSTACK's **NPS of 78** (category-leading for technology advisory) indicates strong advocacy drivers:
- Vendor-agnostic positioning building trust through independence
- Aligned incentives (payment only on successful deals)
- Tangible outcomes (33-day evaluation timelines, documented savings)

#### Activating Referrals

Thank your Promoters and encourage them to share reviews, offer testimonials, or join referral programs so they can amplify their positive experiences; promoters are natural advocates, so give them ways to amplify their positive experiences.

Driving customer advocacy programs involves nurturing satisfied customers into brand champions while empowering support and success teams to effectively tackle challenges faced by less satisfied clients.

Effective models include:
- **Case Study Recognition**: Documenting client wins with measurable outcomes
- **Referral Incentive Programs**: Commission alignment or co-marketing opportunities
- **Community Engagement**: Industry forums, peer roundtables, knowledge-sharing forums

Incentivize customers to share your products and services by offering discounts, free products or other goodies for referring future clients.

For advisory models:
- Vendor partner co-marketing (referral generation creates downstream sales for vendors who compensate advisors)

- Peer network expansion (each engagement creates new stakeholder networks for future opportunities)

### 7. ACCOUNT MANAGEMENT BEST PRACTICES

#### Engagement Models That Work

After the sale is complete, a CSM will work with the customer to ensure long term growth and success, using the high touch post onboarding model and working with the customer through implementation, renewal, and beyond.

Customer success strategies are built on a proactive engagement model, involving constant analysis of customer data, personalized onboarding processes, and regular check-ins to ensure clients are achieving their goals with the product.

For UPSTACK specifically, the advisory model combines:
- **High-Touch Initial Phase**: Discovery → evaluation framework development → recommendation delivery (typically 33 days)
- **Implementation Partnership**: Ongoing vendor management, negotiation support, implementation oversight
- **Strategic Advisory Layer**: Continuous monitoring, renewal guidance, expansion opportunity identification

#### Optimal Engagement Cadence

A detailed engagement plan should outline how you'll support customers at each point, determining the frequency of check-ins, the type of content that will be shared, and the channels used.

Research supports:
- **Monthly**: Account reviews, usage/performance updates, upcoming renewal windows
- **Quarterly**: Strategic planning sessions, emerging technology insights, budget cycle planning
- **As-Needed**: Tactical support during vendor transitions, implementation phases, issue escalations

Factor in deal complexity and customer revenue when assigning accounts; more demanding enterprise clients warrant dedicated nurturing from CSMs so they don't feel neglected.

#### Staying Top-of-Mind

Share insightful and relevant content that educates customers revolving around pain points, industry trends, or other related topics; this approach is nurture-based and establishes your brand as a valuable resource, helping build trust and authority.

Carry out a customer success program that explicitly focuses on their achievements; for example, send a cold email follow-up asking how your service has impacted their metrics or offering assistance to maximize results.

Effective tactics:
- **Value Documentation**: Ongoing tracking of negotiated savings, uptime improvements, cost avoidance
- **Industry Insights**: Vendor consolidation trends, technology roadmap updates, peer benchmarking
