**Key Activities:**

A stakeholder (or multiple) recognizes a business problem worth solving. Usually, the people closest to the problem drive this stage: end users, department heads, or team leads. They surface the pain points and start internal conversations about fixing them.

- Assess current state (systems, costs, performance)
- Identify pain points (cost, reliability, compliance)
- Gather business case rationale

**Decision-Makers Active:** Operations manager, department head, IT troubleshooter (not yet CIO)
**Information Sources:** Internal systems, peer feedback, vendor outreach
**Timeline:** 0–30 days (can be triggered instantly for crisis, or deferred for 6+ months if low-priority)

**Advisory Model Fit:** **MINIMAL—Too Early**
- Buyers are still clarifying the problem internally
- Advisor insertion here looks like "vendor-sponsored research," creating trust barrier
- Exception: MSP relationships already embedded in operations can surface triggers as part of account management

- High customer churn (> 10% annual; indicates dissatisfaction)
- Opaque pricing (bundled licensing; makes TCO impossible to model)
- Poor support reputation (long response times; outdated knowledge base)
- Vendor has fired/replaced exec team recently (instability signal)

## **CONCLUSION: ADVISORY MODEL FIT SUMMARY**

### **The Vendor-Neutral Advisory Model Works When:**

1. ✓ Buyer has **defined a problem** but not yet a solution (Stage 2-3: Solution Scoping & Vendor Discovery)
2. ✓ Buyer faces **multi-stakeholder complexity** (8+ decision-makers; needs single POC)
3. ✓ Buyer values **independence over speed** (willing to invest 60-120 days for due diligence)
4. ✓ Buyer operates in **regulated industry** (security/compliance risks justify advisor scrutiny)
5. ✓ Buyer controls **$1M+ procurement budget** (advisory fee < 2% of deal value = worthwhile)
6. ✓ Buyer has **limited in-house expertise** (no dedicated procurement; no vendor network)
7. ✓ Buyer wants **ongoing lifecycle management** (not one-time procurement, but multi-year vendor governance)

### **The Advisory Model Struggles When:**

1. ✗ Buyer is in **crisis/emergency procurement** (no time for RFP process)
2. ✗ Buyer has already **chosen a vendor** (looking for endorsement, not advice)
3. ✗ Buyer operates in **price-only evaluation mode** (lowest cost wins; advisory complexity seen as friction)
4. ✗ Buyer has **strong internal procurement team** (vendor management is core competency; advisory redundant)
5. ✗ Procurement **< $500K** (advisory fee becomes material % of deal)
6. ✗ Buyer uses **MSP/managed service** for all procurement (vendor selection already delegated)
