## 4. CUSTOMER LIFETIME VALUE ANALYSIS

### LTV Progression for Advisory Relationships

For recurring/retainer-based advisory:
- **Year 1:** Initial contract value
- **Year 2-3:** Expansion revenue + renewal
- **Year 4+:** Multi-service portfolio with lifecycle management premium

In the context of businesses with recurring revenue—including most financial advisory firms—the evaluation of client lifetime value looks quite different. The value is the years, decades, or even lifetime a customer remains engaged

### Predictors of High vs. Low LTV

**High LTV Indicators:**

User expansion—growth in the number of active buyers or approvers per account—is a strong sign of embedded usage

- Demonstrated ROI and measurable outcomes
- Multi-touchpoint relationships across the customer organization

**Low LTV Drivers:**

A weak onboarding process is still the top predictor of churn. Over 20% of voluntary churn is linked to poor onboarding

Customer loss rarely happens due to one dramatic failure. More often, it's slow erosion caused by small, recurring breakdowns in execution, such as unreliable service, confusing processes, or delayed responses. These moments create friction that compounds until the customer quietly tests alternatives

### Measuring CLV Impact

Customer lifetime value is the total amount of revenue a company can expect to earn from one customer over the course of that person's life, less costs for acquisition, serving and retention

For UPSTACK's vendor-reimbursed model:
- CLV = (Average annual revenue per customer × average tenure) - (CS/advisory costs)
- Verified 3.5-year average tenure (from context) suggests strong LTV fundamentals
