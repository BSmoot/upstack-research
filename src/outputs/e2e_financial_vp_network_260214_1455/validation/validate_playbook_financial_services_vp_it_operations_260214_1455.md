## IMPROVEMENT RECOMMENDATIONS

### High Priority

1. **Complete Touch 5 Outbound Copy**: Provide specific email subject line and body copy, plus LinkedIn message template. Consider this touch as a "door reopener" after Touches 1-4, perhaps featuring:
   - Financial services vendor management trend research
   - Relevant regulatory update (DORA guidance, reporting requirements, etc.)
   - Market intelligence (e.g., "top vendor failure points we're seeing across financial services")
   - Natural positioning back toward initial conversation

### Medium Priority

2. **Strengthen UPSTACK Brand Presence in Outbound Sequence**: While the company guidance emphasizes "lead with the problem, not the solution," emails and LinkedIn messages benefit from firm name visibility. Currently:
   - ✓ Phone call mentions "from UPSTACK" (Touch 3)
   - ✓ Signature lines would show firm name
   - ⚠ Touch 1 email subject line doesn't mention firm
   - ⚠ Touch 2 LinkedIn doesn't mention firm name in opener

   **Recommendation**: Consider adding firm identification in Touch 1 subject line or signature, and in Touch 2 LinkedIn opener (e.g., "I'm [Name] from UPSTACK..." or end with "— [Name] at UPSTACK"). This maintains problem-first positioning while establishing firm credibility.

3. **Enhance Vendor-Funded Model Explanation in Outbound**: The model is mentioned in Touches 1-2 but remains understated compared to company guidance emphasis. Consider reinforcing with language like:
   - "our advisory is vendor-funded — you pay nothing" (currently used)
   - Add brief rationale: "Like technology service distributors, vendors reimburse our advisory so you get senior-level expertise without consulting fees" (current playbook omits rationale)
   - This makes the unusual model less likely to trigger skepticism

### Low Priority

4. **Add Specific Vendor Examples**: The playbook references "connectivity provider" generically. Consider adding 1-2 specific vendor categories common in financial services IT operations (e.g., "network connectivity from Equinix/Zayo," "security platforms from Palo Alto/CrowdStrike," etc.) to reinforce market intelligence positioning.

5. **Cite Research Sources in Research Integration Notes**: While industry statistics are appropriately used, adding brief source attribution in the Research Integration Notes section (e.g., "78% increasing IT spending — [Financial Services Research Source X]") would strengthen credibility and help future users understand where claims originate.
