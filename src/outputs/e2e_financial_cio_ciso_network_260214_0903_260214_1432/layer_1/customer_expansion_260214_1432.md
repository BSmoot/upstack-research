## 4. CUSTOMER LIFETIME VALUE ANALYSIS

### CLV Progression Patterns:

If a client generates $20,000 annually in revenue and is retained for 15 years, the total lifetime contribution is $300,000; if annual service costs are $14,000, CLV is $90,000.

For UPSTACK's model:
- **Initial deal LTV**: Single evaluation project revenue
- **Year 1 LTV**: Evaluation + initial expansion project
- **Year 3.5 LTV** (UPSTACK average tenure): Multiple expansions + ongoing advisory retainers

### High vs. Low LTV Predictors:

**High LTV Indicators:**

Customers who refer others are more engaged and loyal, while referred customers tend to stay longer and have higher lifetime value

Regular QBRs are twice as likely to renew, indicating higher LTV trajectory
- Multi-stakeholder relationships (not single point of contact)
- Vendor consolidation alignment (advisor becomes trusted partner for broader portfolio)

**Low LTV Risks:**

Customer loss rarely results from one dramatic failure; more often it's slow erosion from small, recurring breakdowns (unreliable shipment, confusing quote, delayed approval) that compound into disengagement

- Limited adoption of recommendations
- Inadequate communication cadence between engagements

### LTV Metrics to Track:

Net Retention Rate (NRR) is the most comprehensive customer success metric, measuring percentage of revenue retained from existing customers including expansions, upgrades, downgrades, and cancellations.

For advisory relationships: NRR should track expansion revenue from additional evaluations, contract negotiations, and lifecycle management services over base retention.
