### How Compliance Requirements Shape Infrastructure Decisions

Security, compliance, and risk management call for a comprehensive approach when building a digital banking platform, with tools such as the AWS Well-Architected Framework providing a consistent approach to make infrastructures secure, and strict contractual requirements and regular audits or assessments helping make sure the platform adheres to applicable regulations and industry standards.

A single compliance violation can result in millions in fines, regulatory sanctions, loss of operating licenses, and irreparable damage to brand reputation.

For PCI-DSS non-compliance, fines range from $5,000 to $100,000 per month, increased transaction fees, and potential loss of the ability to process card payments—effectively a business death sentence for most organizations.

### Unique Compliance-Related Infrastructure Needs

1. **Audit Trail Infrastructure**:

All security event information must be logged and reviewed, with FFIEC guidelines requiring monitoring of network traffic at the perimeter, and PCI DSS requirement 10 mandating continuous tracking and monitoring of access to network resources and payment data.

2. **Vendor Risk Management Systems**:

Many financial institutions engage third parties to provide products and services, requiring vendor due diligence, and institutions are required to perform ongoing monitoring of the relationship.

3. **Multi-Region Data Residency**: Compliance with jurisdiction-specific rules requires infrastructure in specific geographic zones (e.g., GDPR for EU data, state-specific requirements).

### FFIEC Third-Party Risk Management Guidance (2024 Update)

On June 6, 2023, the Federal Reserve System, the Federal Deposit Insurance Corporation, and the Office of the Comptroller of the Currency issued an Interagency Guidance on Third-Party Relationships: Risk Management, serving as a compass for financial institutions for evaluating and monitoring both existing and new vendors.

**Key Requirements:**
1. **Due Diligence**: Assessment of vendor financial stability, operational capabilities, compliance posture
2. **Ongoing Monitoring**: Continuous assessment of vendor performance, security posture, financial health
3. **Contingency Planning**: Documented plans for service disruption, vendor failure, contract termination
4. **Risk Categorization**: Identify "critical" vendors requiring enhanced oversight

**Compliance Audit Findings (Common):**
- Incomplete due diligence documentation
- Lack of ongoing vendor performance monitoring
- Missing or inadequate contingency plans
- Inadequate security assessments of third parties

**UPSTACK Positioning in Compliance Gap:**

Meeting FFIEC Guidelines for Management of Strategic Vendors requires a vendor management process to measure and audit all strategic vendors in every area of the institution, examining each vendor's current financial status, market segment and risk assessment by industry-experienced professionals, yet most financial institutions do not have internal resources to allocate to this process.

UPSTACK fills this gap by:
1. Conducting initial vendor risk assessments (audit readiness)
2. Establishing ongoing monitoring protocols (FFIEC-aligned scorecards)
3. Managing contingency planning and SLA enforcement
4. Providing documentation and audit evidence
